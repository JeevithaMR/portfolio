About

Who I Am

I'm someone who enjoys solving meaningful business and real-world problems through creativity, leadership, curiosity, and continuous learning. I don't define myself by a degree or a single profession — I'd rather let my work and experiences speak for themselves. Whether it's leading a team, researching a market, or stepping on stage to speak, I bring the same mindset: stay curious, stay adaptable, and keep building.

Most of what I know, I've picked up by actually doing the work — leading college teams through real business simulations, digging into markets no one asked me to research, and saying yes to opportunities that pushed me a little further each time. I'm comfortable switching between roles: the person analyzing a spreadsheet one day, and the one hosting a stage in front of a few hundred people the next.

I think a lot about how businesses actually work — not just the theory of it, but the messy, practical side: how a team stays motivated, how a pitch lands, how a market actually behaves once real people are involved. That curiosity is what pulls me toward new tools, new certifications, and new rooms to walk into.
